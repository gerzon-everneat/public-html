Is Neatr an AI OS,
or an FSM with AI features?

Four parallel investigators read the booking-fe codebase (also the mobile backend). Every claim below traces to a file citation or a founder answer. No vibes.

Scope: 7 capabilities · 5 AI call sites · data-moat · vertical coupling Founder inputs: 8 questions answered Method: DEEP / THIN / MISSING, cited
The verdict
It's an FSM with AI features, not an AI OS.
FSM + AI features
manual decisions, static config AI OS
software makes the operational decisions

The software makes no operational decisions: assignment is manual, job duration is a static default (120 min), scheduling is deterministic, and no captured actual ever changes a future decision. An OS decides; this one presents options and records what humans chose.

Data moat

The data moat — ingredients present, refinery absent

Rich proprietary telemetry is captured on every booking. It's just never fed back into a decision.

Captured (per tenant)
Arrivals + frozen on-time baselines · full status history · travel/lunch time logs · early-clockout reasons · per-space protocol + before/after photos · 1–5 ratings, tips · price breakdown + shadow price-audit
Fed back into decisions
FEEDBACK LOOP: NO
computePricing reads only static config; availabilityLogic has zero references to booking history or ratings. Estimated-vs-actual is recorded and thrown to a display-only readout.

It's a write-only data lake with a display-only readout. That's the single biggest gap between the code and an "AI OS" story.

Coupling

Vertical coupling: HIGH

The primary pricing path is square-footage-and-rooms shaped: productionRate = sqft/hour, tier = sqft brackets, plus sqft surcharges/overflow. Many first-class models hard-code cleaning: protocol, before/after images, ChecklistSpaceType, property-inventory, airbnb*, cleanApprovals.

Escape hatch
Generic time-based core (services/categories/attributes with additionalMinutes, skills, scheduling) is vertical-agnostic. A lawn business could bend sqft to lot size.
Hard wall
Plumbing/HVAC are per-task, not area-priced — they can't onboard today without code changes to the sizing/pricing model. TAM today = cleaning, not "home services".
Three lenses

Three lenses

An operator who churned off two FSM platforms, an investor deciding whether to fund, a builder estimating replication cost. They're built to disagree.

Operator

Runs a fully-booked 15-crew shop
  • The booking widget is real — I own my funnel instead of renting BookingKoala's.
  • Messaging governance lets me control customer comms — usually a black box elsewhere.
  • I don't care it's an "AI OS." I care 15 crews stay booked.
  • No auto-assign = I hand-assign every job. A daily tax, not a nicety.
Verdict: a better-owned FSM for my own shop. "Platform-ness" is irrelevant to me.

Investor

Deciding whether to fund
  • 0 external tenants; the only user is the founder's own business = pre-PMF.
  • Flat SaaS pricing ⇒ a logo-count business. With 0 logos, ARR is $0.
  • "AI OS" over 5 stateless wrappers is a diligence landmine.
  • But the infra depth signals a team that ships hard things.
Verdict: fundable on team + infra, not on the AI-OS story or traction.

Builder

Estimating replication cost
  • Cheap / not a moat: auto-assign, reports, inbound webhooks, the 5 AI wrappers.
  • Expensive / real: availability engine, messaging governance, public widget, outbound outbox.
  • The AI-OS delta is small in effort, large in narrative — and the telemetry already exists.
Verdict: ~6–8 months to rebuild the shipped surface. The AI adds weeks, not defensibility.
↯ Where they materially disagree: is single-tenant dogfooding a strength or a liability?

Operator: strength — running your own payroll on it is the only honest way to harden an FSM.

Investor: liability — a captive user masks whether anyone else will pay.

Builder: neutral — the multi-tenant substrate is already wired; it costs nothing architecturally.

Decision

Stop one thing. Double down on one thing.

✕ Stop

Building the neatlist marketplace / supply layer. The rationale is parity ("BookingKoala has it") — the exact reasoning that manufactures feature bloat. 0 tenants, no two-sided liquidity, all supply is your own crew, and you can't even auto-assign within one crew yet.

✓ Double down

Traction before moat. Win your own BK migration cleanly and land the first 3 external cleaning tenants. With 0 tenants, no feedback-loop engineering matters — proof a second shop runs live bookings is worth more than any model. The feedback loop is your next double-down.

Does the code support folding neatlist into neatr as the supply layer & freezing the marketplace?
Freeze — SUPPORTEDCan't serve non-cleaning, no liquidity either side, parity motive, no single-tenant auto-assign. Keep it frozen until liquidity pressure forces it.
Fold-in as architecture — SUPPORTEDIf you ever build supply, it reuses primitives that already live in neatr (tenant scoping, skills, availability). Folding in > a separate product.
The stated reason — WRONG"BookingKoala has it" is the wrong reason. Freeze it for no-demand / no-liquidity — and let a real unfulfillable booking, not competitor envy, be what unfreezes it.
