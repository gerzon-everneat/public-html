Why we clean the way we do

Works without compromise: human, planet, design.

After cleaning more than a thousand homes ourselves, we noticed something the cleaning industry is built to ignore: the products that actually work are the ones making people sick. Everneat began in 2015 as one of New York’s first eco-friendly cleaning services, and on nearly every job we hit the same wall. The gentle products did not really clean. The ones that did were harsh. Every day, we were choosing between a home that was clean and one that was safe.

That tradeoff was not abstract. Angelo’s asthma got worse with every harsh product he used. When we started hiring, we interviewed more than 500 professional cleaners, and the pattern was impossible to miss: rashes, breathing problems, immune systems worn down by years of exposure. The customers we cleaned for were breathing the same thing in their homes. The industry knew, and kept selling the next bottle anyway.

We were sure there was a better way, because Claudia had already seen one. She trained as an architect, where every surface is a material with its own rules for care. Cleaning, she realized, should work the same way: surface by surface, written down, nothing left to guesswork. That discipline became the Everneat Protocol™, surface-safe, detailed, and efficient.

Angelo set the bar for the other half of the problem. He had worked in high-stakes environments where a mistake was not an option, and he lives with asthma and chemical sensitivity, so he knew exactly what a clean has to deliver to count as healthy: clean air, safe surfaces, and no harsh residue, for the people we clean for and the people who do the cleaning.

Knowing the standard was one thing; the products to meet it did not exist. So we made them. Working with specialized labs, we formulated with the same class of biotechnology European hospitals use, enzymes and probiotics that keep working after the surface is wiped, and no synthetic chemicals. For the first time, the products that cleaned best were also the safe ones.

Today Everneat is one system, a cleaning service and a product line. It is for busy New Yorkers who want a home that is healthy and sustainable, for the teams whose health we will not spend to get there, and for people beyond our service areas who can now buy the products directly. More than 10,000 homes later, the point is the same as it was in 2015: no one should have to choose between a clean home and a safe one.

Claudia and Angelo Zimmermann,
co-founders
